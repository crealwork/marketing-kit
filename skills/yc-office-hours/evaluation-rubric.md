# Evaluation Rubric

## Problem Viability Matrix (Des Traynor)

Place the idea on this 2x2 grid before proceeding with deep dive.

```
              FREQUENT              RARE
         +-------------------+-------------------+
   BIG   |  IDEAL            |  ACCEPTABLE       |
         |  Build this.      |  Can work if      |
         |  Large market,    |  willingness to   |
         |  recurring need   |  pay is high      |
         +-------------------+-------------------+
  SMALL  |  POSSIBLE         |  REJECT           |
         |  Engagement       |  "Typographi-     |
         |  plays, habit     |  cally stunning    |
         |  formation        |  but defunct"     |
         +-------------------+-------------------+
```

If Small + Rare: flag immediately. "This is a small, rare problem. History says these don't work."

## Founder Signal Scorecard

Track during the session. Each signal observed = +1 point.

| # | Signal | How to detect |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Real problem articulated | Describes specific pain, not hypothetical scenario |
| 2 | Named specific customers | People with names/titles, not "small businesses" |
| 3 | Pushed back with evidence | Disagreed on a premise AND provided supporting data |
| 4 | Domain expertise demonstrated | Insider knowledge not available from desk research |
| 5 | Bias for action shown | Already built, sold, or tested something manually |
| 6 | Conviction survived challenge | Belief persisted after hard questions |
| 7 | Contrarian insight identified | Knows something the market doesn't |
| 8 | One-sentence clarity | Explained the business clearly and concisely |
| 9 | Learning velocity visible | Changed approach based on evidence in recent weeks |
| 10 | Solves others' problems | Not just scratching own itch |

**Scoring:**
- 7-10: Strong signals. Recommend PROCEED.
- 4-6: Mixed signals. Recommend TEST FIRST.
- 1-3: Weak signals. Recommend PIVOT or KILL.
- 0: No evidence of viability. KILL.

## Verdict Decision Tree

```
Has the user validated demand with real customers?
+-- YES -> Do they have paying customers or strong retention?
|   +-- YES -> PROCEED. Focus on scaling.
|   +-- NO  -> TEST FIRST. Action = convert interest to payment.
+-- NO  -> Is there a contrarian insight + founder-market fit?
    +-- YES -> TEST FIRST. Action = validate demand this week.
    +-- NO  -> Is the problem Big + Frequent?
        +-- YES -> TEST FIRST. Action = talk to 5 potential customers.
        +-- NO  -> KILL or PIVOT. Problem is too small or too rare.
```

## Marketing Campaign Scorecard

Rate each dimension 1-10. Average = overall campaign score.

| Dimension | 1-3 (Weak) | 4-6 (Okay) | 7-10 (Strong) |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Message Clarity** | Multiple messages competing. Reader confused about what this is. | Clear but generic. Could be any competitor. | One unmistakable message. Reader knows exactly what to do and why. |
| **Audience Fit** | Targeting "everyone" or a vague persona. | Defined segment but not validated. | Specific, named-profile audience with validated pain point. |
| **Channel Strategy** | Using channel because it's trendy, not because audience is there. | Right channel, wrong format or timing. | Audience naturally present. Format matches consumption habits. |
| **CTA Strength** | No clear CTA, or multiple competing CTAs. | One CTA but low urgency or unclear value. | One CTA. Obvious, specific, low-friction. Clear value exchange. |
| **Differentiation** | Looks like every other campaign in the space. Generic AI aesthetics. | Some differentiation in copy or design. | Unmistakably unique. Would stop the scroll. Could not be confused with competitor. |

**Scoring:**
- 8-10 average: Ship it. Monitor and iterate.
- 6-7 average: Revise weak dimensions before shipping.
- 4-5 average: Significant rework needed. Fix the weakest dimension first.
- 1-3 average: Start over. Fundamental positioning problem.

## Known Tarpits

Flag immediately if the idea matches a known tarpit pattern:

| Tarpit | Why it fails |
|--------|-------------|
| Group payments / bill splitting | Every college student thinks of this. Low willingness to pay. Solved "enough" by Venmo/Zelle. |
| Social network for X | Network effects require critical mass. No path to critical mass without existing distribution. |
| AI chatbot with no vertical focus | "ChatGPT wrapper" without unique data, workflow, or integration. |
| Marketplace with no supply strategy | Two-sided marketplaces need supply before demand. If no supply plan, it's a wish. |
| Consumer app requiring behavior change | Changing habits is extremely hard. Need 10x improvement over status quo. |
| "Uber for X" without high frequency | On-demand models need frequent usage to justify infrastructure. |
| Resume builder / job matching | Saturated. Low willingness to pay from candidates. Recruiters already have tools. |

## Anti-Patterns in Pitches

Detect and flag:

| What they say | What it means | Response |
|---------------|---------------|----------|
| "We have no competition" | Either no demand or haven't researched | "If no one else is solving this, why not? Is there actually demand?" |
| "We just need to go viral" | No distribution strategy | "Virality is an outcome, not a strategy. What's the actual plan?" |
| "Our target is everyone" | No focus | "Who is the ONE person? Start there." |
| "We're like Uber but for..." | Cargo-culting, not thinking | "What's YOUR insight? Forget Uber." |
| "We just need more features" | Product isn't resonating | "Features won't fix a positioning problem. Why aren't current users staying?" |
| "Investors love it" | Using investor validation as proxy for customer validation | "Investor excitement is not customer demand. Are users paying?" |
| "We pivoted to AI" | Chasing trend without insight | "What changed about your customer understanding? Just adding API calls isn't a pivot." |
